\begin{abstract}
Deploying deep learning models in edge environments frequently mandates severe dimensionality reduction. However, compressing spatial feature maps into ultra-low-dimensional latent vectors causes classical classifiers to suffer from topological collapse, severely degrading performance. In this study, we investigate whether Hybrid Quantum-Classical Neural Networks (HQCNNs) can overcome this limitation by mapping highly compressed vectors into a complex quantum Hilbert space. We enforce a strict 4-dimensional information bottleneck on a ResNet-18 backbone and evaluate a 4-qubit Variational Quantum Circuit (VQC) against equivalent classical linear and multi-layer perceptron baselines. Through end-to-end optimization on medical imaging benchmarks (BreastMNIST and PneumoniaMNIST), we identify a ``Latent Reshaping'' effect: backpropagated quantum gradients actively adapt classical convolutional filters to output linearly separable, quantum-friendly geometries. The VQC consistently bypassed classical optimization plateaus, achieving superior AUC-ROC across both full-data and severe data-scarcity (1\% and 10\%) regimes. However, robustness evaluations under additive Gaussian noise reveal a fundamental ``Precision Paradox.'' In data-scarce regimes, the high-frequency phase interference granting the VQC its expressivity renders it exceptionally fragile, inducing catastrophic phase decoherence ($\sigma > 0.03$) while classical models degrade gracefully. Crucially, we empirically demonstrate that data abundance acts as a topological regularizer; VQCs optimized on complete datasets neutralize this fragility, maintaining robust decision boundaries that outperform classical architectures under extreme sensor noise. These findings define the critical expressivity-robustness trade-offs governing practical quantum advantage in constrained neural architectures.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
The intersection of quantum mechanics and deep learning has generated significant theoretical interest, primarily driven by the prospect that quantum circuits can represent highly complex data distributions with exponentially fewer parameters than classical frameworks \cite{Schuld2018}. In the Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum (NISQ) era, where fault-tolerant algorithms remain inaccessible, Parameterized Quantum Circuits (PQCs) and Variational Quantum Algorithms (VQAs) have emerged as the standard heuristic models for Quantum Machine Learning (QML) \cite{Benedetti2019}. Theoretical literature suggests that embedding low-dimensional classical data into a high-dimensional quantum Hilbert space allows these circuits to approximate non-linear continuous functions with superior parameter efficiency \cite{Jerbi2023}. However, demonstrating a practical, empirical ``quantum advantage'' in standard, unconstrained computer vision tasks has proven notoriously elusive.

A highly viable, yet underexplored, domain for quantum advantage lies in edge computing under severe information constraints. Modern clinical pipelines increasingly demand the deployment of sophisticated diagnostic models—such as deep Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)—to portable, low-power medical devices. These edge deployments are strictly bottlenecked by computational, thermal, and bandwidth limitations \cite{edge_medical}. To satisfy these constraints, high-resolution spatial feature maps are often aggressively compressed into ultra-low-dimensional latent vectors prior to classification or remote transmission. 

Standard classical dimensionality reduction techniques inevitably falter under severe compression ratios. When rich, non-linear manifolds—such as those representing ambiguous tumor opacities or pneumonic infiltrates—are forced into restricted Euclidean spaces ($\mathbb{R}^d$), critical topological features become irreversibly entangled. Consequently, classical linear or multi-layer perceptron (MLP) classification heads applied to these bottlenecks suffer from ``topological collapse.'' The network simply lacks the spatial degrees of freedom required to draw separating hyperplanes, causing diagnostic performance to abruptly hit a premature optimization plateau \cite{pca_limit}.

Hybrid Quantum-Classical Neural Networks (HQCNNs) present a mathematically elegant paradigm to bypass this topological entanglement \cite{Henderson2019}. By utilizing a classical CNN backbone for generalized feature extraction and a Variational Quantum Circuit (VQC) as the classification head, HQCNNs can theoretically remap the collapsed classical vector $z \in \mathbb{R}^d$ into an exponentially larger complex Hilbert space $|\psi\rangle \in \mathbb{C}^{2^n}$, where $n$ is the number of qubits. While recent studies have utilized HQCNNs for anomaly detection \cite{Senokosov2024}, the behavior of these highly expressive circuits under the simultaneous pressures of extreme dimensionality bottlenecks, severe data scarcity, and analog sensor noise remains largely undefined. 

In this study, we benchmark fundamental quantum versus classical behaviors under strict architectural constraints. We isolate the expressivity of the classification heads by imposing a severe 4-dimensional information bottleneck ($d=4$) on a pre-trained ResNet-18 backbone. Utilizing standard medical imaging datasets (BreastMNIST and PneumoniaMNIST), we conduct a rigorous comparative analysis of classical linear layers, classical MLPs, and a 4-qubit VQC.

Our core contributions empirically establish critical boundaries for practical quantum advantage in constrained neural architectures:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Latent Reshaping and the Bottleneck Gap:} We empirically demonstrate that forcing classical classifiers through a $4$-dimensional bottleneck causes immediate topological collapse. In contrast, through end-to-end optimization, quantum gradients backpropagate into the classical feature extractor, actively reshaping the classical manifold into a quantum-friendly geometry. The quantum hybrid consistently bypassed classical plateaus, achieving the highest mean AUC-ROC in both data-abundant (up to 0.9787) and severe data-scarcity regimes (10\% BreastMNIST and 1\% PneumoniaMNIST).
    \item \textbf{The Precision Paradox:} Through rigorous Gaussian noise-injection stress tests, we uncover a fundamental operational vulnerability. The high-frequency phase interference patterns responsible for the VQC's superior expressivity render it exceptionally fragile in data-scarce environments. At moderate sensor noise ($\sigma = 0.10$), the data-starved quantum model suffers catastrophic phase decoherence (F1 score collapsing to $0.1452$), while the classical linear model acts as a stabilizing low-pass filter (maintaining an F1 of $0.3581$), establishing a direct trade-off between geometric expressivity and analog robustness.
    \item \textbf{Data Abundance as a Topological Regularizer:} We identify a powerful mitigation mechanism for quantum phase decoherence. We empirically prove that optimizing the VQC on a complete, 100\% data regime neutralizes the fragility of the Precision Paradox. The abundance of data acts as a massive topological regularizer, locking the quantum rotation angles and allowing the VQC to maintain robust decision boundaries that consistently outperform classical architectures even under extreme sensor degradation ($\sigma = 0.20$).
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related Work}

\subsection{Information Bottlenecks and Quantum Expressivity}
The Information Bottleneck principle \cite{Tishby2015} models representation learning as a strict trade-off between compressing input data and preserving predictive mutual information. In edge-computing medical deployments, this bottleneck is deliberately restricted to satisfy bandwidth and privacy constraints \cite{edge_medical}. However, at ultra-low dimensions, classical linear and non-linear (MLP) reductions frequently suffer from topological collapse, destroying the separability of complex clinical manifolds \cite{pca_limit}.

To circumvent this, theoretical literature suggests encoding classical vectors into high-dimensional quantum states. Schuld \textit{et al.} \cite{Schuld2021} established that Parameterized Quantum Circuits (PQCs) operate as partial Fourier series, capable of mapping low-dimensional inputs into highly non-linear continuous functions. Similarly, Abbas \textit{et al.} \cite{Abbas2021} proved that Quantum Neural Networks possess a remarkably high ``effective dimension,'' granting them superior capacity over classical networks. Our research empirically bridges this theory to medical vision, proving that embedding a severely constrained $d=4$ vector into a complex Hilbert space actively bypasses classical topological collapse—a phenomenon we formally define as the ``Bottleneck Gap.''

\subsection{Hybrid Transfer Learning and Latent Geometry}
Directly processing high-resolution medical imagery using pure quantum states remains intractable due to the limited qubit counts of current NISQ hardware. To circumvent this, hybrid transfer learning architectures have become the standard paradigm \cite{Mari2020}. Early approaches, such as Quanvolutional Neural Networks \cite{Henderson2019}, utilized quantum circuits as localized feature extractors. Recent medical applications have successfully inverted this approach, pairing classical Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to extract hierarchical spatial features with quantum classification heads to process the resulting latent vectors \cite{Senokosov2024}. 

While these studies demonstrate parameter efficiency, they predominantly treat the classical backbone as a static, frozen feature extractor. Our work extends this literature by investigating End-to-End optimization. We demonstrate a ``Latent Reshaping'' effect, wherein allowing gradients to backpropagate from the quantum head—utilizing the parameter-shift rule \cite{Mitarai2018, Schuld2019_gradients}—actively forces the classical residual layers \cite{resnet} to geometrically reorganize ambiguous textures into a quantum-friendly, linearly separable manifold.

\subsection{Noise Fragility and the Precision Paradox}
The operational robustness of quantum models is currently a subject of intense debate. The majority of existing QML literature focuses strictly on \textit{hardware} noise—such as depolarizing channels and measurement errors—and their role in inducing barren optimization plateaus \cite{Wang2021}. While Lu \textit{et al.} \cite{Lu2020} demonstrated the theoretical vulnerability of quantum classifiers to adversarial input perturbations, their resilience to organic, analog sensor noise in edge-constrained medical pipelines is rarely benchmarked against classical equivalents.

In classical biological and artificial neural networks, moderate input noise can occasionally trigger ``stochastic resonance,'' allowing the network to maintain or even improve signal detection by smoothing the loss landscape \cite{McDonnell2011}. In this study, we contrast this classical graceful degradation with quantum phase decoherence. We introduce the ``Precision Paradox'': the precise, high-frequency Fourier mapping that grants VQCs their superior expressivity renders them catastrophically fragile to analog Gaussian noise in data-scarce environments. Furthermore, we contribute to the robustness literature by empirically identifying data abundance as a topological regularizer capable of neutralizing this quantum fragility.

\section{Theoretical Framework}

To formalize the mechanism underlying the ``Bottleneck Gap'' and subsequent Latent Reshaping, we mathematically define the representational capacities of both the classical and quantum classification heads under a severe information bottleneck constraint.

\subsection{The Classical Information Bottleneck and Topological Collapse}
Let the pre-trained, truncated ResNet-18 backbone act as a generalized feature extractor mapping an input image $x \in \mathbb{R}^{H \times W \times C}$ to a mid-level hierarchical latent representation $h \in \mathbb{R}^{d_{in}}$. By immobilizing the network prior to the terminal residual block (`layer4`), the global average pooling layer yields a feature vector where $d_{in} = 256$. 

To simulate the strict bandwidth and computational constraints of edge-device transmission, we impose an extreme information bottleneck via a dense linear projection down to a dimension $d_B \ll d_{in}$, where $d_B = n = 4$:
\begin{equation}
    z = W_{comp} h + b_{comp} \quad, \quad z \in \mathbb{R}^{d_B}
\end{equation}

In our classical baselines, this ultra-compressed vector $z$ is fed either directly into a linear classification head or into a multi-layer perceptron (MLP) utilizing a Gaussian Error Linear Unit (GELU) \cite{hendrycks2016gelu} activation to test classical non-linear expressivity:
\begin{align}
    \hat{y}_{lin} &= W_{clf} z + b_{clf} \\
    \hat{y}_{mlp} &= W_{clf}(\text{GELU}(z)) + b_{clf}
\end{align}

Because the compression matrix $W_{comp}$ is strictly linear, highly entangled and non-linear data manifolds residing in $\mathbb{R}^{256}$ undergo irreversible geometric overlap when projected onto $\mathbb{R}^4$. The subsequent classical classifiers, fundamentally restricted by the ultra-low dimensionality of $z$, frequently suffer from ``topological collapse.'' They lack the spatial degrees of freedom—and consequently the topological capacity—to draw the complex, multi-dimensional hyperplanes necessary to separate overlapping clinical classes, resulting in early optimization plateaus and high loss ceilings.

\subsection{Quantum Hilbert Space Embedding and Expressivity}
The Hybrid Quantum-Classical architecture (HQCNN) aims to resolve this topological entanglement by replacing the classical classification head with a Variational Quantum Circuit (VQC). The identical $4$-dimensional bottleneck vector $z$ is mapped into a quantum state. 

To prevent anomalous periodic phase-wrapping during embedding, the continuous classical vector is first scaled into a strictly bounded rotational phase-space:
\begin{equation}
    \tilde{z} = \tanh(z) \cdot \left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right) \quad, \quad \tilde{z} \in \left[-\frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}\right]^{d_B}
\end{equation}

We employ an angle embedding strategy, applying localized Pauli-$Y$ rotations to initialize the quantum subsystem from the ground state $|0\rangle^{\otimes n}$:
\begin{equation}
    |\psi(\tilde{z})\rangle = \bigotimes_{i=1}^{n} R_y(\tilde{z}_i) |0\rangle
\end{equation}

This operation constitutes the core geometric advantage: while the classical input $z$ contains only 4 variables mapped in a constrained Euclidean space $\mathbb{R}^4$, the resulting encoded quantum state $|\psi(\tilde{z})\rangle$ resides in a complex Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}$ of dimension $2^{n}$. For $n=4$ qubits, the data is geometrically expanded into a $16$-dimensional complex vector space ($\mathbb{C}^{16}$). 

The state is subsequently evolved via a parameterized unitary $U(\theta)$, instantiated as sequences of Strongly Entangling Layers comprising arbitrary single-qubit rotations and cyclic CNOT entangling cascades. To extract classical predictions, we measure the expectation value of the Pauli-$X$ observable ($\sigma_x$) across all $n$ wires, producing a continuous quantum feature vector $v_q \in [-1, 1]^n$:
\begin{equation}
    v_q^{(i)} = \langle \psi(\tilde{z}) | U^\dagger(\theta) \sigma_x^{(i)} U(\theta) | \psi(\tilde{z}) \rangle \quad \forall i \in \{1, \dots, n\}
\end{equation}

The final classification logit is obtained via a dynamically learned, weighted summation of these quantum expectation values:
\begin{equation}
    \hat{y}_{q} = \sum_{i=1}^n w_i v_q^{(i)} + b_{logit}
\end{equation}

This framework establishes the mathematical foundation for the observed ``Bottleneck Gap.'' The classical models are restricted to linear or mildly non-linear hyperplanes in $\mathbb{R}^4$. In contrast, the VQC evaluates complex expectation values across entangled states in $\mathbb{C}^{16}$. This Fourier-accessible quantum feature space provides the necessary expressivity to resolve and separate decision boundaries that remain hopelessly entangled in the constrained classical latent space.

\section{Methodology}

To rigorously test the geometric expressivity hypothesis, we designed a unified Hybrid Transfer Learning pipeline. The architecture is explicitly constructed to isolate the classification heads (Classical vs. Quantum) by forcing all spatial information through an identical, highly constrained latent bottleneck, guaranteeing strict structural parity.

\subsection{Convolutional Feature Extraction and Targeted Unfreezing}
We utilize the ResNet-18 architecture \cite{resnet} as the foundational feature extractor. Pre-trained on the ImageNet dataset, the network's early convolutional blocks act as robust generalized texture and edge detectors. To adapt the network to medical imaging modalities while adhering to our extreme dimensionality constraints, we truncated the network by discarding `layer4`, the global average pooling, and the fully connected layer. 

The resulting truncated backbone processes input images up to `layer3`, outputting a mid-level hierarchical spatial tensor which is subsequently passed through an `AdaptiveAvgPool2d(1, 1)` and flattened into a feature vector $h \in \mathbb{R}^{256}$.

To prevent catastrophic forgetting and mitigate severe overfitting in the $1\%$ and $10\%$ data-scarcity regimes, we employed a selective fine-tuning strategy. The early residual blocks (`layer1` and `layer2`) were permanently immobilized. During the end-to-end Latent Reshaping phase, only the terminal residual block of the truncated backbone (`layer3`) was unfrozen. This permits the network to dynamically update high-level, domain-specific spatial hierarchies (e.g., radiological opacities) while leveraging the stability of the pre-trained generalized filters.

\subsection{The Information Bottleneck Layer}
The core constraint of our experiment is the Information Bottleneck. The $256$-dimensional feature vector $h$ is passed through a dense linear projection layer:
\begin{equation}
    z = W_{comp} h + b_{comp} \quad, \quad z \in \mathbb{R}^{4}
\end{equation}
where $W_{comp} \in \mathbb{R}^{4 \times 256}$ and $b_{comp} \in \mathbb{R}^{4}$. This severe compression ratio ($64:1$) forces the neural network to encode the entirety of the medical image's diagnostic variance into just four continuous variables.

\subsection{Classical Classification Baselines}
To ensure structural fairness, the classical classification heads were designed to match the $4$-dimensional constraint of the quantum circuit. We established two classical baselines:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Classical Linear:} Projects the bottlenecked vector $z$ directly to a classification logit via a single dense layer ($W_{clf} \in \mathbb{R}^{1 \times 4}$), representing a strictly linear decision boundary.
    \item \textbf{Classical MLP:} Introduces a Gaussian Error Linear Unit (GELU) \textbf{\cite{hendrycks2016gelu}} activation in the latent space before the final linear projection, serving as the benchmark for classical non-linear expressivity under severe dimensionality constraints.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Quantum Classification Head (VQC)}
In the quantum hybrid variant, the classical head is replaced by a Continuous-Variable to Discrete-Qubit interface, a Variational Quantum Circuit (VQC), and a classical post-processing layer. The quantum pipeline utilizes PennyLane \cite{pennylane} for state-vector simulation and executes in four distinct phases:

\subsubsection{Data Scaling and Bounding}
Because quantum rotation gates are periodic modulo $2\pi$, raw continuous features can trigger ``phase-wrapping'' anomalies (where vastly different classical values map to identical quantum states). To prevent this, the classical bottleneck vector $z$ is scaled non-linearly into a strictly bounded phase-space using a hyperbolic tangent function:
\begin{equation}
    \tilde{z} = \tanh(z) \cdot \left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right) \quad, \quad \tilde{z} \in \left[-\frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2}\right]^{4}
\end{equation}
This bounds the rotational angles to a continuous manifold without periodic overlap.

\subsubsection{Quantum State Preparation}
The bounded classical vector $\tilde{z}$ is embedded into a 4-qubit quantum subsystem initially in the ground state $|0\rangle^{\otimes 4}$. We utilize Angle Embedding via independent single-qubit Pauli-$Y$ rotations:
\begin{equation}
    |\psi_{in}\rangle = \bigotimes_{i=1}^{4} R_y(\tilde{z}_{i}) |0\rangle
\end{equation}

\subsubsection{Parameterized Ansatz}
The core of the quantum expressivity lies in the parameterized unitary operation $U(\theta)$. We employ $L=2$ repetitions of Strongly Entangling Layers. Each layer applies arbitrary single-qubit rotations $R(\alpha, \beta, \gamma)$ to every qubit, followed by a cyclic cascade of controlled-NOT (CNOT) gates to generate deep, multi-qubit entanglement. The total number of trainable quantum parameters is $\theta \in \mathbb{R}^{L \times n \times 3} = \mathbb{R}^{24}$, where $n=4$.

\subsubsection{Measurement and Post-Processing}
We measure the expectation value of the Pauli-$X$ observable ($\sigma_x$) on all four wires, yielding a continuous classical measurement vector $v_q \in [-1, 1]^4$:
\begin{equation}
    v_{q, i} = \langle \psi_{in} | U^\dagger(\theta) \sigma_{x}^{(i)} U(\theta) | \psi_{in} \rangle \quad \forall i \in \{1, 2, 3, 4\}
\end{equation}
This quantum measurement vector is dynamically processed through a final learned classical weight array ($W_{obs} \in \mathbb{R}^{1 \times 4}$) and bias to produce the final classification logit $\hat{y}_{q}$. 

\subsection{Optimization and Differential Training Protocol}
Due to the disparate gradients inherent to hybrid architectures, standard uniform optimization frequently results in barren plateaus or catastrophic classical forgetting. We implemented a differential learning rate strategy optimized via Adam \textbf{\cite{kingma2014adam}} : the unfrozen classical `layer3` parameters were updated conservatively ($\eta = 10^{-4}$), while the classical linear/MLP heads and the quantum parameters were optimized at $\eta = 10^{-3}$ and $\eta = 5 \times 10^{-3}$, respectively. 

To mitigate the severe class imbalances present in clinical datasets, models were optimized using a Binary Cross-Entropy with Logits Loss (BCEWithLogitsLoss) dynamically weighted by the negative-to-positive class ratio of the subsampled training set. A `ReduceLROnPlateau` scheduler was employed to stabilize final convergence. All hybrid models were trained end-to-end by backpropagating classical gradients through the quantum circuit utilizing the parameter-shift rule \textbf{\cite{Mitarai2018, Schuld2019_gradients}}.

\section{Experimental Setup}

To empirically validate the Latent Reshaping and Precision Paradox hypotheses, we engineered a rigorous, deterministic testing environment utilizing standardized medical imaging benchmarks.

\subsection{Datasets and Severe Scarcity Regimes}
We evaluated the architectures across two distinct imaging modalities from the MedMNIST v2 collection \cite{medmnist}. To conform to the pre-trained ResNet-18 feature extractor requirements, the native $28 \times 28$ grayscale images were interpolated to $224 \times 224$ pixels via anti-aliased resizing, duplicated across three RGB channels, and normalized using standard ImageNet distribution statistics.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{BreastMNIST:} Comprises breast ultrasound images categorized into benign and malignant tumors. This dataset presents a challenging geometric task characterized by low-contrast, highly ambiguous textures.
    \item \textbf{PneumoniaMNIST:} Comprises pediatric chest X-ray images classified into normal and pneumonia-infected lungs, representing a more structurally distinct imaging modality.
\end{itemize}

To rigorously evaluate the parameter efficiency of the VQC against the classical baselines, we established two extreme training environments. First, models were trained on the full available datasets (100\%) to establish upper-bound performance baselines. Second, to simulate the severe data scarcity frequently encountered in novel or rare-disease clinical settings, we aggressively constrained the training sets via deterministic subsampling: \textbf{10\% for BreastMNIST} (yielding approximately 54 training samples) and \textbf{1\% for PneumoniaMNIST} (yielding approximately 58 samples). The validation and test sets remained at 100\% volume across all trials to guarantee strictly standardized comparative evaluation.

\subsection{Optimization Protocols and Class Mitigation}
Integrating a randomly initialized quantum circuit with a pre-trained classical backbone introduces significant optimization instability, often leading to barren plateaus or catastrophic forgetting. We systematically mitigated this through the following protocols utilizing the Adam optimizer:

\textbf{1. Frozen-Backbone Ablation:} To establish a baseline of inherent head expressivity with zero latent gradient variance, the entire ResNet-18 backbone was strictly immobilized. Only the dense bottleneck projection and classification heads were trained.

\textbf{2. End-to-End Fine-Tuning:} To enable Latent Reshaping, the terminal residual block (`layer3`) was unfrozen. We implemented a strict Differential Learning Rate strategy: the backbone was updated at $\eta_{backbone} = 10^{-4}$ to carefully preserve ImageNet-derived spatial filters, the classical classification heads at $\eta_{clf} = 10^{-3}$, and the VQC parameters at $\eta_{q} = 5 \times 10^{-3}$ to ensure adequate traversal of the quantum phase space.

Across both regimes, models were trained for exactly 50 epochs without early stopping to observe true saturation plateaus. To counteract severe clinical class imbalances, we utilized Binary Cross-Entropy with Logits Loss, dynamically scaling the positive class weight by the negative-to-positive sample ratio of the specific scarcity regime.

\subsection{Evaluation Metrics and Threshold Locking}
Because medical datasets exhibit skewed distributions, raw classification accuracy is a highly misleading metric. Models were primarily evaluated using the Area Under the Receiver Operating Characteristic Curve (AUC-ROC), providing a threshold-invariant measure of the classifier’s geometric separability.

However, to evaluate the practical deployment viability of the decision boundaries, we also calculated the F1 Score. Crucially, rather than utilizing a static $0.5$ logit boundary, the optimal classification threshold was dynamically calculated on the validation set by maximizing the Precision-Recall curve. This optimal threshold was subsequently ``locked'' and explicitly applied to the test set to ensure realistic clinical evaluation.

\subsection{Sensor Degradation and RNG Parity Protocol}
To evaluate the ``Precision Paradox'' and benchmark operational resilience, we subjected the fully optimized models to a sensor degradation stress test. Pristine test images $X_{test}$ were corrupted with additive Gaussian noise:
\begin{equation}
    \tilde{X}_{test} = \text{clamp}(X_{test} + \epsilon, \ 0.0, \ 1.0) \quad, \quad \epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2)
\end{equation}
The noise standard deviation $\sigma$ was incrementally swept across $[0.0, 0.2]$. The `clamp` function ensures the spatial tensors respect strict biological and hardware sensor limits. 

To guarantee absolute methodological parity, the random number generator was re-seeded as a deterministic function of $\sigma$. This ensures that the Classical Linear, Classical MLP, and Quantum VQC models faced the exact same spatial noise tensors per image. To accurately measure decision boundary decay rather than dynamic re-calibration, the optimal Precision-Recall threshold calculated on pristine data ($\sigma=0.0$) was strictly locked across all subsequent noise levels.

\subsection{Software and Hardware Framework}
Classical deep learning operations and gradient autograd engines were implemented in PyTorch. The quantum circuits were constructed and evaluated using PennyLane \cite{pennylane}, utilizing the \texttt{pennylane-lightning} plugin for high-performance state-vector simulation.

\section{Results I: Expressivity and Latent Reshaping}

The primary objective of these initial evaluations was to isolate the information-retention capacity of the competing classification heads under the strict $d=4$ bottleneck constraint, and to observe how classical feature manifolds adapt to quantum gradients. 

\subsection{Ablation Study: The Frozen Bottleneck Baseline}
To establish a foundational baseline and isolate the classification heads from classical gradient variance, we first evaluated the architectures under the frozen-backbone regime. By immobilizing the entire ResNet-18 backbone, the classification heads were forced to map raw, unoptimized ImageNet features directly to clinical classes. 

On BreastMNIST under the severe 10\% scarcity regime, the classical linear head achieved a mean Test AUC of $0.7127$, slightly outperforming the Quantum VQC ($0.7022$) and the Classical MLP ($0.6721$). This dynamic is mathematically consistent with the geometry of the frozen latent space. Because pre-trained ImageNet filters are structurally misaligned with ultrasound textures, the $4$-dimensional bottleneck output acts as highly ambiguous spatial noise. The classical linear model optimally responds to this unstructured noise by drawing a blunt, static hyperplane. In contrast, the VQC utilizes a high-frequency trigonometric embedding strategy (Equation 3). When fed unoptimized spatial noise, the parameterized rotation angles struggle to find a stable, continuous manifold, resulting in minor phase misalignment and sub-optimal convergence.

\subsection{The Bottleneck Gap and Latent Reshaping}
To test whether the quantum circuit could overcome this initial misalignment, we transitioned to the end-to-end Latent Reshaping regime by unfreezing the terminal residual block (`layer3`). This permitted the quantum classification head to backpropagate gradients directly into the classical convolutional filters. The empirical results, summarized in Table \ref{tab:peak_auc}, reveal a profound reversal in architectural dominance.

On the BreastMNIST 10\% scarcity regime, unfreezing the backbone caused the VQC's performance to surge, achieving a mean AUC of $0.7770$ and decisively eclipsing both the Classical Linear ($0.7659$) and Classical MLP ($0.7635$) baselines. This statistically significant delta visually formalized in Figure \ref{fig:bottleneck_gap}, confirms the ``Latent Reshaping'' hypothesis. While the VQC struggles with static noise, its quantum gradients possess a geometrically superior capacity to drive classical convolutional layers toward a linearly separable, quantum-friendly topology, even with merely 54 training samples. 

This quantum dominance in severe scarcity was identically replicated on the PneumoniaMNIST 1\% regime (approx. 58 images), where the VQC achieved a superior mean AUC of $0.9271$, breaking the optimization plateau established by the classical baselines ($0.9228$).

\subsection{Expressivity Dynamics and Topological Collapse}
Analysis of the epoch-by-epoch optimization trajectories further elucidates the mechanical failure of classical dimensionality reduction. As illustrated in the training loss and validation AUC curves (Figure \ref{fig:dynamics}), both classical linear and MLP baselines exhibit rapid early saturation. 

For instance, in the BreastMNIST 10\% regime, classical architectures hit a hard mathematical lower bound in BCE loss roughly by Epoch 15, resulting in a flatlined validation AUC. This phenomenon is a direct visualization of classical ``topological collapse.'' When highly entangled spatial hierarchies are compressed into a strictly $4$-dimensional Euclidean space ($\mathbb{R}^4$), classical networks exhaust their spatial degrees of freedom, rendering the overlapping classes mathematically inseparable. 

Conversely, by embedding the identical $4$-variable bottleneck into a $16$-dimensional complex Hilbert space ($\mathbb{C}^{16}$), the VQC bypasses this dimensional restriction. The quantum optimization curves (Figure \ref{fig:dynamics}, Orange) demonstrate continuous, stable learning trajectories well beyond the classical plateau. These results empirically validate the theoretical premise that high-dimensional quantum Hilbert spaces can successfully separate decision boundaries that are topologically collapsed in equivalent classical frameworks.

\section{Results II: Robustness and The Precision Paradox}

While the Hybrid Quantum-Classical model demonstrated superior expressivity in pristine environments, clinical edge deployment demands strict resilience against analog sensor degradation. To systematically evaluate this resilience, we subjected the models to additive Gaussian noise $\mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2)$ across the normalized test sets, utilizing strict RNG parity to ensure identical noise tensor distribution across architectures. Crucially, optimal Precision-Recall thresholds were calculated strictly at $\sigma=0.0$ and locked for all subsequent noise evaluations, isolating the true decay of the learned decision boundaries.

The resulting degradation curves (Figure \ref{fig:precision_paradox}) reveal a fundamental conflict between mathematical expressivity and geometric stability, a phenomenon we term the ``Precision Paradox.''

\subsection{The Glass Cannon Effect under Data Scarcity}
In severe data scarcity regimes, the quantum architecture operates as a highly expressive but catastrophically fragile instrument. 

On the BreastMNIST 10\% test set at baseline ($\sigma = 0.0$), the pristine VQC achieved the highest performance, registering an F1 score of $0.8665$. However, it exhibited extreme sensitivity to subsequent input perturbations. At a moderate noise threshold of $\sigma = 0.10$, the quantum hybrid experienced a catastrophic crash, with its F1 score plummeting to $0.1452$. 

Conversely, the classical models degraded much more gracefully. At the identical $\sigma=0.10$ noise level, the Classical Linear and MLP baselines maintained substantially higher F1 scores ($0.3581$ and $0.3095$, respectively).

This fragility is mathematically inherent to the VQC's expressivity mechanism when starved of data. The Strongly Entangling Layers rely on precise, high-frequency trigonometric phase angles (Equation 3) to generate constructive interference. When trained on only 54 samples, the model overfits to the narrow, pristine spatial distribution. The injected Gaussian variance $\mathcal{N}(0, 0.10^2)$ propagates through the unfrozen ResNet, causing an amplified shift in the latent vector $z$. This slight angular perturbation misaligns the entire quantum state, destroying the learned interference pattern and inducing rapid phase decoherence. The classical linear model, functioning essentially as a low-pass topological filter, is structurally immune to this specific high-frequency decoherence.

\subsection{Data Abundance as a Topological Regularizer}
The most profound finding of our robustness analysis occurred when testing the VQC optimized on the full 100\% training regimes. 

When the BreastMNIST 100\% models were subjected to the exact same noise sweep, the Glass Cannon effect completely vanished. At the previously fatal noise threshold of $\sigma=0.10$, the fully-trained VQC maintained a highly robust F1 score of $0.8214$, successfully outperforming both the Classical Linear ($0.8186$) and MLP ($0.8141$) baselines. This resilience was maintained even at extreme degradation ($\sigma=0.20$), where the Quantum F1 score stabilized at $0.8146$. 

This empirically proves that data abundance acts as a powerful topological regularizer for quantum classification heads. The massive increase in training variance forces the quantum gradients to construct broader, flatter minima in the phase space. This prevents the rotation angles from locking onto highly specific, high-frequency spatial artifacts, neutralizing the fragility of the Precision Paradox and allowing the VQC's geometric advantage to survive in noisy environments.

\subsection{Phase-Space Saturation and the ``Zombie State''}
At extreme noise levels ($\sigma = 0.20$), we observed a unique mathematical failure mode exclusive to the hybrid architecture, driven by the $\tanh(z)$ continuous-to-discrete bounding function.

In the classical network, extreme spatial noise simply overwhelms the latent signal, flattening the output distributions toward random guessing. However, the quantum model's inputs are rigidly constrained by $\tilde{z} = \tanh(z) \cdot (\pi/2)$. High-variance classical noise frequently pushes the latent features deep into the asymptotic tails of the hyperbolic tangent function, saturating the output at exactly $\pm \pi/2$. 

Consequently, the quantum rotation gates are forced to evaluate fixed, absolute boundary angles. The VQC ceases to evaluate a continuous phase space; instead, the saturated inputs collapse the quantum circuit into a quasi-discrete topological state. This ``Zombie State'' frequently yields static measurement artifacts—such as the VQC artificially maintaining an AUC of $0.6118$ with an impossibly low probability standard deviation of $0.0057$ on the noisy Breast 10\% test set. This confirms that the non-linear boundaries required to prevent quantum phase-wrapping introduce unique saturation-based failure modes under high-noise conditions, functionally distinct from standard classical degradation.

\section{Discussion}
The empirical findings of this study illuminate and formalize the complex operational realities of Hybrid Quantum-Classical Neural Networks. By isolating the classification heads behind a severe $d=4$ information bottleneck, we successfully demonstrated that Variational Quantum Circuits possess a fundamental geometric advantage over equivalent classical classifiers. 

Our end-to-end optimization results establish the phenomenon of ``Latent Reshaping.'' The VQC's ability to map compressed representations into a high-dimensional complex Hilbert space is not merely a passive projection; rather, the quantum gradients backpropagated through the network successfully force the classical convolutional layers to organize ambiguous spatial textures into linearly separable, quantum-friendly topologies. This ``Bottleneck Gap'' allowed the VQC to consistently bypass classical optimization plateaus, achieving superior AUC-ROC scores across both full-data and severe scarcity regimes.

However, our robustness analysis reveals that this extreme expressivity is inherently coupled with a critical operational vulnerability. In data-starved environments (e.g., BreastMNIST 10\%), we identified the ``Precision Paradox.'' The precise, high-frequency trigonometric phase interference required to generate the VQC's superior decision boundaries renders it highly fragile to organic spatial perturbations. Minor analog sensor noise ($\sigma = 0.10$) induced rapid phase decoherence, transforming the quantum head into a ``Glass Cannon'' while the rugged, low-pass classical linear model degraded gracefully. 

Crucially, our study establishes the mechanism to neutralize this fragility. We empirically demonstrated that data abundance acts as a powerful topological regularizer. When optimized on the complete training distribution, the VQC maintained its geometric expressivity while entirely shedding its noise vulnerability, maintaining an F1 score of $0.8146$ even under extreme Gaussian degradation ($\sigma = 0.20$). These findings formalize an Expressivity-Robustness-Data trade-off in NISQ-era quantum machine learning: the theoretical advantages of quantum data embedding are empirically valid under severe bottlenecks, but deploying them safely in noisy environments strictly requires the regularizing force of abundant training data.

\subsection{Limitations and Future Directions}
While these empirical results definitively establish the Latent Reshaping effect and the Precision Paradox, we acknowledge several structural boundaries within our experimental scope that warrant future investigation.

First, the $d=4$ information bottleneck was an extreme constraint explicitly designed to force classical topological collapse. We hypothesize that as the classical bottleneck dimension scales upward (e.g., to $d=16$ or $d=32$), the classical non-linear MLP will recover sufficient geometric degrees of freedom to resolve the target medical manifolds, gradually narrowing the observed Bottleneck Gap. Establishing the exact dimensionality threshold where classical spatial capacity overtakes quantum parameter efficiency will be critical for edge-deployment scaling equations.

Second, given that data abundance successfully regularizes quantum phase decoherence, future algorithmic research must focus on artificially synthesizing this regularization in data-scarce environments. Investigating quantum-native noise-injection protocols during the training loop (e.g., perturbing the Pauli rotation angles within the Strongly Entangling Layers) may allow VQCs to achieve robust, flat minima without requiring vast datasets.

Finally, our quantum circuits were strictly evaluated using high-performance state-vector simulations. Deploying this hybrid architecture on physical Quantum Processing Units (QPUs) will introduce intrinsic hardware decoherence (e.g., thermal relaxation, crosstalk, and CNOT gate errors). How this quantum hardware noise interacts with—and potentially compounds—the classical input-driven phase decoherence observed in our sensor degradation tests is currently undefined. Bridging the gap between input noise and hardware noise via error-mitigated data embedding strategies remains the ultimate hurdle for deploying highly expressive VQCs in analog clinical environments.

\section{Conclusion}
In this paper, we investigated the viability of Hybrid Quantum-Classical Neural Networks (HQCNNs) for medical image classification under severe edge-computing constraints. We empirically establish that while extreme linear dimensionality reduction forces a hard topological performance ceiling on classical models, Variational Quantum Circuits (VQCs) offer a mathematically profound mechanism to bypass this limitation. Through end-to-end optimization, we demonstrated the ``Latent Reshaping'' effect: backpropagated quantum gradients actively force classical convolutional filters to reorganize ambiguous medical textures into linearly separable, quantum-friendly geometries. This phenomenon allows the VQC to consistently outperform classical baselines in both data-abundant and severe data-scarcity regimes.

However, our robustness analysis under additive sensor noise defines a critical boundary condition for practical quantum advantage, which we term the ``Precision Paradox.'' In data-scarce environments, the high-frequency trigonometric phase interference that grants VQCs their superior expressivity simultaneously renders them catastrophically fragile to spatial perturbations. In these regimes, the VQC suffers rapid phase decoherence while the classical model acts as a rugged, low-pass filter. 

Crucially, we prove that this fragility is not an absolute quantum limitation, but a regularizable topological artifact. By optimizing the VQC under data abundance, the quantum circuit achieves geometric stability, completely neutralizing the Glass Cannon effect and maintaining highly robust decision boundaries against extreme analog noise.

Based on these empirical findings, we define the following operational paradigms for edge-constrained clinical AI:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Data-Abundant Deployments:} HQCNNs are strictly recommended. The abundance of data acts as a topological regularizer, allowing the VQC to achieve both maximum diagnostic expressivity and superior noise resilience.
    \item \textbf{Data-Scarce, High-Noise Deployments (e.g., analog field scanners):} Classical MLPs remain the safest baseline due to their graceful degradation, until novel quantum noise-injection protocols can be developed to artificially regularize the VQC phase space during optimization.
\end{itemize}

Ultimately, while NISQ-era quantum classifiers operate as highly sensitive precision instruments, their unparalleled capacity to resolve entangled topological features—and their ability to actively reshape classical latent geometries—confirms their transformative potential for the next generation of constrained edge intelligence.
